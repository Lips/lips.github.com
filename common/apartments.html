Housing is expensive in general, and very expensive right now. You can find rooms for under 1000, but they’re not common in neighborhoods where someone coming from out of town is likely to feel comfortable. That’s not to say they aren’t safe.

Craigslist is generally what everyone uses.

Jersey, Westchester, and long island are all much cheaper than NYC proper. Some locations would require a vehicle, but it is possible to get a place within walking distance of mass transit.
There many differences between those options, culture, style, history - but what would be a principal importance is the transit point which connects them to New York City.
Jersey is connected to New York by the PATH train which means that locations on Christopher Street 14th and 34th are very accessible.
Metro North, coming from West Chester, New Rochelle and a ton of other little towns, stops at Grand Central.
The Long Island Railroad feeds into Penn Station.
